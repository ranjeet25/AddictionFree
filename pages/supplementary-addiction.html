supplementary addiction

"Breaking free from all addictions - overcome the cycle of supplementary addiction."

Supplementary addiction, also known as secondary addiction, is a phenomenon where an individual develops a new addiction as a result of trying to cope with an existing addiction. This often occurs when someone is trying to overcome an addiction to drugs, alcohol, or another substance, and they begin to engage in other addictive behaviors to compensate for the loss of their primary addiction.

Treatment for supplementary addiction typically involves addressing both the primary and secondary addictions simultaneously. This may involve therapy, counseling, support groups, and other forms of behavioral and cognitive interventions to help individuals develop healthy coping mechanisms and overcome their addictive behaviors.
