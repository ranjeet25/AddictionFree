online shopping addiction

"Shop smart, not addictively - break free from online shopping addiction."

Online shopping addiction, also known as compulsive buying disorder, is a behavioral addiction characterized by an excessive and uncontrollable urge to shop online. It is a type of impulse control disorder that can have serious negative consequences on an individual's personal, social, and financial life.
Online shopping addiction can start innocently, with a desire to buy a few things online. However, it can quickly escalate into a compulsive behavior that takes up a significant amount of an individual's time, energy, and money. The constant need to shop can lead to financial problems, including debt, bankruptcy, and loss of savings. It can also cause social and emotional problems, including relationship conflicts, isolation, depression, and anxiety.
The ease of online shopping makes it particularly easy for individuals to develop an addiction. With just a few clicks, individuals can access a vast array of products and services, all from the comfort of their own home. This convenience can make it difficult for individuals to resist the urge to shop and can exacerbate their addiction.
