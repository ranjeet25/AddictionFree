Video Game Addiction

"Game smart, not addictively - break free from video game addiction."

Video game addiction is a behavioral addiction characterized by excessive and compulsive gaming. It is a type of impulse control disorder that can have negative consequences on an individual's personal, social, and academic or occupational life.
People with video game addiction may prioritize gaming over important life activities, such as work, school, or socializing, leading to isolation, poor academic or occupational performance, and relationship problems. Treatment may involve therapy, behavioral modification techniques, and self-help strategies to help individuals regain control over their gaming habits and improve their overall well-being.
Video game addiction can affect individuals of any age and can have both physical and psychological effects, such as poor sleep patterns, weight gain, and increased aggression. The immersive and rewarding nature of video games can make it difficult for individuals to resist the urge to play for extended periods, leading to a cycle of addictive behavior. It is important for individuals to recognize the signs of video game addiction and seek professional help if necessary to overcome this addiction and improve their quality of life.
